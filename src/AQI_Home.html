Decoding the Air We Breathe

1. What is Air Pollution?

  • Presence of harmful substances in the atmosphere.
  • Includes outdoor pollution (vehicles, factories, crop burning) and indoor pollution (smoke, poor ventilation).

2. What is AQI (Air Quality Index)?

  • AQI communicates current or forecasted pollution levels.
  • Scale: 0 to 500 (higher = worse).
  • Major pollutants: PM2.5, PM10, NO₂, SO₂, CO, O₃.

3. Causes of Air Pollution

  • Human-made: Vehicle emissions, industrial discharge, construction dust, burning fossil fuels, waste burning.
  • Natural: Wildfires, dust storms, volcanic activity.

4. Effects on the Present Generation

  • Respiratory diseases: asthma, bronchitis, lung cancer.
  • Heart issues, eye irritation.
  • Decreased productivity due to illness.
  • Environmental effects: smog, acid rain, poor visibility.

5. Effects on Future Generations

  • Poor lung development in children.
  • Higher risk of cognitive and developmental disorders.
  • Long-term environmental damage.
  • Harm to biodiversity and climate.

6. Real-Life Solutions

Individual Level

  • Use public transport, carpool, bike, or walk.
  • Shift to electric vehicles and clean fuels.
  • Avoid burning garbage or leaves.
  • Grow air-purifying plants.

Community Level

  • Create green zones and urban forests.
  • Control dust/emissions at construction sites.
  • Promote recycling and waste segregation.
  • Raise community awareness.

Government Level

  • Enforce pollution laws.
  • Invest in renewables and public transit.
  • Regulate crop burning.
  • Educate the public via media campaigns.
